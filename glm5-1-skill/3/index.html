OASIS

THE DEPTH WITHIN

Descend

The mind is water. Stillness reveals its depth.

Oasis is not a retreat from the world. It is a descent into the world beneath the world — the vast, luminous interior that opens when the surface of the mind becomes perfectly still. At the edge of an ancient caldera lake, surrounded by volcanoes and silence, we practice the oldest technology known to consciousness: the art of going deep.

Three Depths of Practice
01 — Surface

Breath Awareness

The surface is where all descent begins. Breath awareness anchors the wandering mind to the rhythm of the body, creating the first stillness. At Lake Atitlan, each breath mirrors the lake's own gentle rise and fall — a synchronization between seeker and water. This is the practice of arriving, of becoming present enough to notice that the water was always still.

Depth
Shallow
02 — Mid-Depth

Sensory Withdrawal

Below the surface, the world grows quiet. Sensory withdrawal — Pratyahara — is the practice of releasing attachment to external stimuli, allowing the senses to turn inward. Sound becomes distant, light becomes diffused, and the boundary between self and water begins to dissolve. This is where meditation transforms from technique into experience, from doing into being.

Depth
Intermediate
03 — Abyss

Formless Contemplation

In the abyss, there is no form, no reference, no shore. Formless contemplation is the dissolution of the observer into the observed — the point where the meditator becomes the meditation. The water does not reflect; it absorbs. Time ceases to move linearly. The practitioner does not seek stillness; stillness is all that remains. This is the deepest gift of Oasis.

Retreat Specification
Location
Lake Atitlan
Guatemala, Central America. 1,562 metres above sea level. Caldera waters fed by volcanic springs, revered by the Maya as the navel of the world.
Duration
10 · 17 · 24
Days. Three paths of descending length and deepening practice. The ten-day foundation. The seventeen-day immersion. The twenty-four-day transformation.
Capacity
16 Seekers
Intentionally small. Each retreat holds a maximum of sixteen practitioners to preserve the resonance of concentrated stillness.
Alignment
New Moon
2027. Every retreat begins on the new moon — the darkest sky, the deepest water, the most receptive silence.
