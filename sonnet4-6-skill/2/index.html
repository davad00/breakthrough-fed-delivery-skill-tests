Philosophy

"The temple
is within"

The Balinese do not separate the sacred from the everyday. Every meal is an offering. Every sunrise, a ceremony. Every breath, a return. Oasis was built on this truth — that retreat is not escape, but reunion.

Rooted in the ancient Usada healing traditions of Bali, our philosophy weaves together the four pillars of Balinese medicine: herbs and earth, breath and sound, sacred ritual, and the grace of stillness. You do not come here to fix yourself. You come to remember what was never broken.

Experiences

01

Fire Ceremony at Dawn

Before the island wakes, gather at the open-air pavilion. A Balinese high priest leads the Agni Hotra — the ancient fire ceremony — as the sky shifts from ink to ember. Offerings of coconut husk, camphor, and flowers dissolve into flame. Silence holds everything.

Daily · 05:30 · Ceremonial Pavilion

02

Stone Bath Immersion

Our signature bathing ritual draws from the Balinese tradition of tirta — holy water as medicine. Soak in hand-carved volcanic stone pools fed by a mountain spring, surrounded by night-blooming jasmine and the sound of water on ancient rock. Oil of white ginger and sandalwood is pressed into the skin.

By Appointment · 90 Minutes · The Stone Garden

03

Sound & Silence — Gamelan Meditation

Lie on a bed of woven palm and let the sound find you. Live gamelan — the ancient bronze orchestra of Bali — is played not for performance but for vibration. Each tone resonates through the body, dissolving the boundary between self and sound. This is not music. This is frequency.

Three Times Weekly · 19:00 · The Inner Temple

Sanctuary Spaces

Twelve suites. No two alike. Each built around a single material truth.

Every suite at Oasis was designed by Balinese master craftsmen using a single dominant material — volcanic stone, aged teak, river reed, fired terracotta. Your room is not a room. It is a meditation on one element of the earth. Beds face east, toward the sunrise. Baths are always outdoors.

  • 12 Private Suites, each unique
  • Outdoor stone soaking tubs, all suites
  • Open-air sleeping pavilions available
  • No screens, no clocks, no minibar
  • Private garden with fire altar
  • Daily in-room offerings by our pemangku
  • Minimum 3-night stay required
  • All meals included, Balinese cuisine
