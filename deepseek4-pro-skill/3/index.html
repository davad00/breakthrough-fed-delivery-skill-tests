✦ Presence

The air itself is practice.

Suspended above the mist-laden valleys of the Pacific Northwest, Oasis exists where cloud meets canopy. Here, the only instruction is to breathe. The only practice is presence. Every surface, every window, every open-air pavilion is designed to dissolve the boundary between interior and the living world beyond.

✦ Spaces

Rooms that breathe.

Eighteen floating suites suspended among ancient Douglas firs. Each features retractable walls opening to forest canopy, a private meditation platform extending into the mist, and760 a lavender stone bath fed by natural geothermal springs. The architecture is light — glass, pale timber,040 linen — as if the building itself is exhaling.

✦ Practices

Four breaths.
One stillness.

Morning Fog — guided breathwork at dawn in open-air pavilions. Forest Bathing — the Japanese practice of shinrin-yoku through ancient trails. Cloud Meditation — floating practice on geothermal pools. Deep Listening — sound meditation with Himalayan bowls and forest silence.

✦ Arrive

Let go. Breathe in.

Oasis welcomes eighteen guests at a time. Your stay begins the moment you step onto the forest path. No check-in desk. No paperwork. Just a warm tea, a deep breath, and the trees.
