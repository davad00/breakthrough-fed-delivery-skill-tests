Architecture of Stillness

The Space

Designed by architect Miro Tanaka in collaboration with indigenous landscape consultants, Oasis occupies 48 acres of protected high-desert terrain. Every structure is oriented to celestial and geological landmarks.

Materials were sourced within 200 miles. No artificial lighting intrudes past 9pm. The architecture does not impose on the landscape — it dissolves into it.

48 Acres
The Resonance Hall

A 4,000 square foot structure of rammed earth and glass, tuned to the acoustic properties of the surrounding canyon walls. Used for evening sound ceremonies and group meditation.

Private Casitas

Twenty individual adobe dwellings, each with a private walled garden and outdoor shower. Minimal furnishing, maximum presence. No television. No clocks visible. Every surface intentional.

The Living Kitchen

Chef-crafted plant-based cuisine prepared from our on-site garden and regional farms. Each meal is approached as ceremony — an extension of the practice, eaten in silence or soft conversation.
